Gratitude

Introduction

Ferris Bueller once said "Life moves pretty fast. If you don't stop and look around once in a while, you could miss it."

It can be so easy to get engrossed in situations around that have fallen through and forget to show appreciation for the things that we have. One can only see the beauty of life and bask fully in it when an individual leads life on the path of gratitude and appreciation.

To help on the gratitude journey, here are ways to lead a life filled with gratitude.

1. Avoid being picky: appreciate everything.

More often than not, we tend to reserve gratitude for the things we connsider as "big". We forget that everything is indeed big and that the classification of events into big or small is relative to the individual. The habit of being grateful starts with appreciating every good thing in life and recognising that there is nothing too small for you to be grateful for.

2. Find gratitude in challenges and be mindful.

Gratitude is not only about being thankful for positive experiences. In fact, sometimes thinking about negative or difficult situations can help to really nail down what you have to be thankful for. It is advisable to take time out of the day to take inventory of about five to ten things to be thankful for. The trick is that you need to picture it in your mind and sit with that feeling of gratitude in your body.

Other ways include:

I really could go on about ways of living a life filled with gratitude, rather than bore us all, I'll run through them very quickly.

  • Keep a gratitude journal.
  • Practice the act of givivng back to others.
  • Express gratitude to the people you care about.
  • Spend time with loved ones.
  • Improve your happiness in other areas of your life.

Impacts of gratitude

S/N Impacts Explanation
1. Gratitude changes perspective. Life is all about perspective. Whatever we focus on, we gravitate towards.
2. It improves the quality of an individual's life. You’re the sum of all your parts, and it’s gratitude that can help to benefit each of those small parts.
3. Living wiith an attitude of gratitude fills a person with happiness. It’s far easier to go about your day in a state of appreciation when you’re grateful than when you’re not. When we’re grateful for things, it’s a natural occurrence to be happier about life. You realize the things you have rather than the things you don’t.
4. Gratitude reduces a person's innermost fear. One clear way that gratitude can change your life is by reducing your fears because it’s hard to be fearful and grateful at the same time. Fear is what occurs when we’re left to dwell on topics that we feel are out of our control.
